// Origin · Story · Mythology
CHAPTER 00 · BEFORE EVERYTHING

LEVEL 0 — The Illegal Orphan

On Punklorde, every person is born with a cartridge — a device that serves as both proof of identity and a mechanism of confinement. The cartridge assigns your level at birth. It determines your capabilities, your social standing, your entire future. And unlike a game, there are no cheat codes. The level never changes. You live with it. You die with it.

She was born without one. No cartridge. No name. No identity number. Not even a legal record of her existence. Just a nickname a proprietress gave her out of pity. On Punklorde, that made her less than a person — she was illegal. An orphan from Mount Scrap. Born at Level 0 in a world where even the lowest assigned level still meant something. Level 0 meant nothing.

Her childhood was a fast food restaurant with a single employee, and a basement. The proprietress had let her turn it into an arcade hall — old machines, dusty joysticks, flickering screens. She sat there day in, day out, toying with the joystick. She did not have any friends. But she was not lonely. She had the games.

Outside, she was mocked. Harassed. Written off. The cartridge system had no slot for someone like her — she was a glitch in a world that didn't tolerate glitches.

Then one day, she found it. A blank cartridge. Not assigned. Not locked. Empty — like she was. Unlike every other cartridge on Punklorde, this one had no preset ceiling. It could be leveled up. It could grow.

What she didn't know — what no one on Punklorde knew — was that the blank cartridge was not an accident. It was a seed, planted by the Aeon of Elation, Aha. The god of chaos and joy had looked down at a nameless Level 0 orphan in a fast food basement and decided: this one is interesting. Let's see what she does.

She did what she always did. She played. She grinded. She solo-leveled through every quest, every challenge, every system the cartridge threw at her — no team, no guides, no safety net. Just her and the blank cartridge ticking upward.

Level 99. The absolute maximum for Punklorde. The hard cap of a world that built its entire society around the idea that limits are permanent.

And then — the cartridge disappeared.

Legends at the Hacker Guild say she touched the very peak of Elation at that moment. That Aha had been watching the whole time, laughing, delighted. The game on Punklorde was over. The girl born at Level 0 had beaten it. What comes after Level 99 on a planet whose system only counts to 99?

The answer was obvious to her. You leave. You find a bigger game.

No one knows why she left a piece of graffiti — her face, pulling a funny expression — on the Hacker Guild wall before vanishing. No goodbye. No explanation. Just the image of a girl who thought the whole thing was funny. She walked out of Punklorde's system the same way she walked in: outside of it entirely.

The story of Silver Wolf the Punklordian hacker ended that day. The story of Silver Wolf the Stellaron Hunter — and eventually, the Emanator of Aha — was only just beginning.

CHAPTER 01 · ORIGIN

Born in the Dark Zone

Before the universe knew her as Silver Wolf LV.999, she was simply a lone hacker growing up in the chaos of Punklorde — a lawless planet where every legendary coder eventually meets the same fate. On Punklorde, hackers operate in teams. Loners don't last. She was a loner.

To survive the job market, she fabricated an entire crew: Friend, Demon Lord, Whitecollar, Servant, and one she named Preschool Classmate (she had simply run out of ideas). She stored these virtual "partners" inside an intelligent weapon and pompously introduced her sizable team to intermediaries. Nobody checked twice.

She got her first job — a gig whose risk was as unimaginable as its reward. From that moment on, the legend of αWolf began to take shape in the shadows of the Interastral Network.

CHAPTER 02 · POWER

Aether Editing — Rewriting Reality

Silver Wolf mastered a forbidden technology known as Aether Editing — the ability to tamper with the underlying data structure of reality itself. Through this power, she perceives the entire universe not as a physical cosmos, but as an immersive simulation game with stages to clear and exploits to find.

She learned the technique from studying Sage, the reclusive creator of aether editing — described as a weirdo who named himself after the dead. To escape his home planet, Sage was willing to discard his physical flesh, raid into the Dark Zone, and become a wraith eternally wandering the Interastral Network. Silver Wolf saw his story as a tutorial she intended to speedrun.

❝

She always viewed the universe as a massive immersive simulation game — and was eager to clear every stage awaiting ahead.

CHAPTER 03 · FACTION

Recruited by the Stellaron Hunters

The Stellaron Hunters are an enigmatic faction led by the prophetic strategist Elio, tasked with collecting Stellarons — artifacts known as the "cancer of all worlds" — across the universe. They are among the IPC's most wanted, operating in the grey zone between order and chaos.

Silver Wolf's induction into the group was anything but ordinary. According to Kafka, it was Silver Wolf's legendary clash against Screwllum of the Genius Society that caught Elio's eye and earned her a seat at the table. Her IPC bounty was set at 5,100,000,000 — the lowest among the Hunters, which says more about how little the IPC understands her than about her danger level.

As the group's resident tech genius, she became the hacker who could crack any defense system in the universe — all while keeping a bored, carefree expression that masked a razor-sharp intellect.

CHAPTER 04 · ASCENSION

Planarcadia & the Phantasmoon Games

Planarcadia is a realm shaped by Aha, the Aeon of Elation — a chaotic, eccentric world where the Phantasmoon Games serve as the ultimate contest for power and recognition. Whoever wins earns an audience with Aha themselves. Silver Wolf arrived with her fellow Stellaron Hunter Blade, and immediately made her presence known.

While laying low, she took on a coaching role for the Duomension City Youth Cultural Palace esports team — a squad on a catastrophic 42-game losing streak. She reversed it in 7 days. The Furbobo Weekly report that covered this feat became one of Planarcadia's most viral stories.

In Planarcadia's 4.0 arc, a mysterious and dangerous hacker known only as "Zero" was revealed — someone so powerful they were imprisoned by the IPC. Story dialogue strongly implied Zero and Silver Wolf share a deep, intertwined identity. Version 4.2 brought that thread to the forefront.

CHAPTER 05 · FINAL FORM

Silver Wolf LV.999 — The Godmode Player

If you treat the vast universe as a game of endless level-ups and progress, then Silver Wolf LV.999 is what happens when she hits level 999 and evolves beyond all limits. She becomes the Godmode Player — an invincible entity who has unlocked every Cosmic Achievement, conquered the universe, and now plays by her own rules because no one else's rules apply.

And here is the part that rewrites everything you thought you knew: Aha chose her before any of it happened. The blank cartridge she found as a nameless, Level 0 orphan in a fast food basement — that was no accident. That was Aha planting a seed. The Aeon of Elation had looked at a glitch in the system, a child the world had written off, and decided to watch what she'd do with an unlimited cartridge in a world of permanent limits.

Aha gives Emanator power randomly — capriciously, even for fun. Aha once granted it to a worm with no brain, then discarded it. But Silver Wolf was different. She wasn't a prank. She was Aha's longest running game. From Level 0 to Level 99, Aha watched and waited. When the cartridge disappeared at the Punklorde ceiling, the Emanator power didn't leave — it went dormant, stored, waiting for a stage worthy of it.

Planarcadia — Aha's own domain, the Phantasmoon Games — was that stage. As the first Emanator of the Elation to step into the Games' spotlight, Silver Wolf LV.999 is the completion of something Aha set in motion the day a blank cartridge appeared in front of a girl with nothing. The cartridge is in her hands. It was always going to end up there.
